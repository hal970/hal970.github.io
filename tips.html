Do's and Don'ts

  1. There's no tipping in Japan. In fact, it's taken as an insult if you tip.

  2. Talking on the phone in public transport is huge no-no. You CAN be kicked off with no second chances.

  3. Be courteous, and polite. Even if you don't speak Japanese, body language says alot in Japan.

  4. Meat is pricey in Japan, they're sold by the gram.

  5. Convenience stores generally have everything a person can need, from chargers, real food, spare clothing, the list goes on and on. Very helpful in pinches and budget conscience stays.

  6. Plan ahead, public transport in Japan are almost always on time to the minute, and one missed connection could cost you.
  7. If you're travelling around, within, or between major cities all the time the JR Pass is a great investment.

  8. Cars and people run on the LEFT side of the road, keep this in mind since some placces in Japan do not have sidewalks.

  9. Vending machines are everywhere in Japan, but generally it's a better idea to buy the drink in bulk within stores and take them on the go from the fridge (of where you're staying).

  10. Cherry blossom season is from March to the end of April, and generally other tourists will want to travel during this time.

  11. Japan's rainy season during June to late August, humidity is above the 90s at all time, tempertures also reach the 90s in Fahrenheit and stay that way at night.
